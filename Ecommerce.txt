

E-Commerce Application

-- User Registration and Login

When a customer visits an e-commerce website for the first time, they must create an account by providing personal information such as their name, email address, phone number, and password. This information is stored in the company's database.

Returning customers can simply log in using their registered credentials to access personalized services and continue shopping without creating a new account.

-- Why Registration and Login are Important

* Identify genuine users.
* Store customer information securely.
* Save shopping cart and wishlist across devices.
* Track order history.
* Provide personalized product recommendations.
* Enable faster checkout.
* Protect customer accounts from unauthorized access.

Features of an E-Commerce Application

i) Wishlist

Purpose: Save products for future purchase.

Why: Customers can bookmark products they like and purchase them later without searching again.

---

ii) Add Products to Cart

Purpose: Store selected products before checkout.

Why: Customers can collect multiple items and purchase them together in a single order.

---

iii) Increase or Decrease Product Quantity

Purpose: Modify the number of units of a product in the shopping cart.

Why: Customers can adjust quantities before placing the order.

---

iv) Place Orders from Anywhere

Purpose: Purchase products online from any location.

Why: Customers do not need to visit a physical store, making shopping more convenient.

---

v) Multiple Payment Options

Purpose: Support different payment methods such as:

* UPI
* Debit Card
* Credit Card
* Net Banking
* Digital Wallets
* Cash on Delivery (COD)

Why: Customers can choose the payment method that best suits their preferences.

---

vi) Order Tracking

Purpose: Display the current status of an order.

Examples include:

* Order Confirmed
* Packed
* Shipped
* Out for Delivery
* Delivered

Why: Customers can monitor the progress of their orders in real time.

---

vii) Notifications and Product Recommendations

Purpose:

* Send order updates.
* Notify users about offers and discounts.
* Recommend products based on browsing history, wishlist, previous purchases, and user preferences.

Why: Customers stay informed and discover products they are more likely to purchase.

---

viii) Delivery and Product Returns

Purpose: Deliver products to the customer's preferred address and allow returns when necessary.

Why: Customers can shop from anywhere and return products that are damaged, defective, or do not meet their expectations.

---

ix) Customer Support, Refunds, and Replacements

Purpose: Provide assistance with order-related issues.

Customers can:

* Contact customer support
* Request refunds
* Replace damaged or incorrect products
* Resolve payment-related problems

Why: Ensures customer satisfaction and builds trust in the platform.

---

x) Offers, Coupons, Vouchers, and Gift Cards

Purpose: Provide promotional discounts and rewards.

Why: Customers save money and are encouraged to make additional purchases.

---

xi) Product Comparison

Purpose: Compare products from different sellers based on:

* Price
* Ratings
* Reviews
* Delivery time
* Seller reputation

Why: Helps customers make informed purchasing decisions without manually visiting multiple seller pages.

---

Why Do E-Commerce Applications Keep Users Logged In?

E-commerce applications often keep users logged in for several days or weeks to provide a smooth and personalized shopping experience.

Reasons

* Avoid repeated login every time the application is opened.
* Keep the shopping cart and wishlist synchronized across devices.
* Remember saved addresses and payment preferences.
* Provide personalized product recommendations.
* Allow users to continue shopping from where they left off.
* Increase customer engagement and encourage repeat purchases.

Since e-commerce platforms generally involve **lower security risk** than banking applications, they prioritize **convenience** while still protecting user accounts using secure technologies such as encrypted connections (HTTPS), secure cookies, and authentication tokens.

---

Why Banking Applications Do Not Keep Users Logged In

Banking applications handle highly sensitive financial information such as:

* Bank account details
* Card information
* Transaction history
* Money transfers
* Personal financial records

For this reason, they prioritize **security over convenience**.

Most banking applications:

* Automatically log users out after a period of inactivity.
* Require PIN, password, fingerprint, or Face ID frequently.
* Ask for OTP verification before sensitive transactions.
* Re-authenticate users before adding beneficiaries or transferring money.

---

Why?

A compromised banking session can result in:

* Unauthorized money transfers
* Identity theft
* Financial fraud
* Permanent financial loss

Therefore, banking systems follow a **Zero Trust Security Model**, where every important action requires the user's identity to be verified instead of assuming that a previously logged-in session is always trustworthy.

The guiding principle is:



# Authentication vs Authorization

| Authentication                                                              | Authorization                                                             |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Verifies the identity of a user.                                            | Determines what an authenticated user is allowed to access or perform.    |
| Answers "Who are you?"                                                      | Answers "What are you allowed to do?"                                     |
| Usually performed using a username, password, OTP, fingerprint, or Face ID. | Performed after successful authentication based on roles and permissions. |
| Happens first.                                                              | Happens after authentication.                                             |

Example

A customer logs into Amazon.

* Authentication: The system verifies the customer's email and password.
* Authorization:  The authenticated customer is allowed to view products, add items to the cart, place orders, track deliveries, and manage their own account, but cannot access another customer's orders or modify administrative settings.

This separation ensures both security (only legitimate users can log in) and access control (users can perform only the actions they are permitted to perform).
